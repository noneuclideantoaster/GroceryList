My Bird Feeding Grocery List

Seed

Seed Type Birds Attracted
  • Black Oil Sunflower
    		•
  • Any seed-eating bird. While out of the shell is generally preferred, offer seeds in the shell during caching season.
  • Safflower
    		•
  • Most songbirds. Larger birds do not have the correct beak anatomy to crack the shell. Squirrels find safflower bitter, and will leave feeders containing it alone.
  • Peanuts
    		•
  • Jays and woodpeckers particularly like peanuts. Offer in the shell during caching season.
  • Millet
    		•
  • Offer for ground-feeding birds such as mourning doves. When placed in feeders, birds who dislike millet will throw it on the ground where birds who prefer it will eat it.

    • Seasonal Food

    Food Type Benefits
  • Suet
    		•
  • Suet is boiled beef kidney fat. In the winter, it offers extremely high amounts of fat and protein, which birds need to survive harsh weather, lesser food resources, and the consequences of molting. Suet comes with a variety of added ingredients for different species of birds. Most birds will eat and benefit from suet.
  • Mealworms
    		•
  • Mealworms are sold in a variety of fashions: either live, or roasted/dried. They provide extremely important protein for birds who supplement their diets with insects, and can be taken back to nests for baby birds.
  • Fruit
    		•
  • Many birds enjoy fresh and dried fruit. In the summer, leave jams and ripe oranges out for orioles. Furthermore, if you leave a rotting banana out in the open during the summer, many hummingbirds will eat the fruit flies.
  • Nectar/Sugar Water
    		•
  • Hummingbird and oriole nectar is easily made at home, and provides valuable nutrients for these pollinators.

    • Bird Accessories

    Accessory Type Purpose
  • Bird Bath
    		•
  • Birds require access to fresh water year-round. Consider investing in a heated bird bath during winter months. Birds further like moving water over stagnant, so consider investing in a fountain or water jiggler.
  • Squirrel/Racoon Baffle
    		•
  • Attaching a squirrel or raccoon baffle to a bird-feeding pole will prevent either from eating seed or damaging feeding aparatuses.
  • Rain Coverings
    		•
  • A wide variety of bird feeders have optional rain coverings to protect seed from the elements. These are valuable accessories for both humans and birds.
  • Crash Deterrents
    		•
  • Birds will unfortunately fly into glass windows when startled or otherwise confused. These collisions frequently cause serious internal injuries. Putting decorative decals on windows, and ensure your feeder is far enough from your windows, can help keep them safe.
